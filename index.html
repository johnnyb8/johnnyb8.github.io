About Me

I'm 20 years old and I grew up in Ottawa before moving to St. John's for school. I am an active person and spend my spare time hiking, biking, playing guitar, and playing sports when I have the time.

Hockey and rugby are my main sports and I was on many successful teams during and after high school. I played a leadership role as captain and learned how to work as part of a team and also motivate the other members of the team.

Work

I'm currently doing my second Co-op work term at Nokia networks in Ottawa. It's a terrific workplace with challenging work and a great team atmosphere. My team provides 3rd level support for Nokia installations around the world using one of their successful enterprise level software solutions entitled NSP NFM-P. I have learned a lot about the product itself, and have also had a chance to hone my problem solving, troubleshooting, and programming skills. During my time at Nokia I have provided numerous presentations to both peers and management staff about the work I am doing.

The Ottawa Nokia campus employs about 60 co-op students and I am one of 2 students on the internal Co-op council which Nokia developed to be a voice for the the co-op students.

School

I graduated high school with Honours and was an Ontario Scholar with a French Immersion certificate.

With an aptitude for science, math and troubleshooting it was a logical decision to pursue a career in engineering. Recognizing that the future in most industries would be built around automation and computing I chose Computer Engineering as my discipline.

Choosing Memorial University of Newfoundland (MUN) was an easy decision. With my sister completing her Master of Science degree at MUN and my mother being from Newfoundland I had spent a lot of time on the island and I loved it.

In my first year in residence, I was fully engaged in all activities and actively participated in all of the the engineering d-days! Although I had a LOT of fun, I did the work and was rewarded with good marks.

That first year I also provided math tutoring for a local community association and really enjoyed helping the folks there. In second year I was a math tutor again and worked at MUN helping students with their math studies. It was very rewarding.
